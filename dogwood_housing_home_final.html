Fairfax County and the Environment

In the face of global warming, it is more important than ever to keep our eco-footprint in mind, especially when buying a house in the most populated county of Virginia. With over 50% of residents driving a car as their primary mode of transportation along with 200,000+ buildings (the majority of which are residential), the implications towards climate change cannot be ignored.

There is HOPE

However, Fairfax County is working towards the reduction of carbon emissions by adopting "greener" energy practices. Of course, sustainability is just one of many factors to consider when buying a house.
